# UKAIRS Submission and 2.5-Year PhD Plan

## Summary

Use the Friday deadline, **June 26, 2026**, as a focused 2-page Emerging Research submission, not a mini full paper. The strongest angle is:

**Auditing whether medical VQA and agentic VLM workflows are faithful to visual evidence in GI endoscopy.**

This fits your current work: PaliGemma LoRA fine-tuning on Kvasir-VQA-x1, preliminary attention/Grad-CAM explainability code, and your reading around Medico 2025, Kvasir-VQA-x1, MedHEval, and EndoAgent/EndoCogniAgent.

## UKAIRS 2-Page Piece

Target **Track B: Life & Health**, with secondary relevance to **Synergistic Human-AI Collaboration** and **Algorithms, Agents & Theory**.

Use this structure:

1. **Motivation**
   Explain that GI VQA accuracy is not enough for clinical safety because answers and explanations can look plausible while being visually unfaithful.

2. **Gap**
   Position the gap between:
   Medico/Kvasir-VQA-x1-style VQA benchmarks, MedHEval-style hallucination evaluation, and EndoAgent-style agentic endoscopy systems. The missing piece is evaluation of the full evidence chain: answer, explanation, tool output, memory update, and validation step.

3. **Research Questions**
   Use three concise RQs:
   - Do GI VQA explanations correspond to visual evidence used by the model?
   - Do agentic workflows improve grounding or simply make reasoning traces more persuasive?
   - Can perturbation, counterfactual, and tool-corruption tests reveal failures hidden by answer accuracy?

4. **Current Work**
   State only what is defensible:
   - Fine-tuned `google/paligemma-3b-pt-224` with QLoRA/LoRA on Kvasir-VQA-x1.
   - Built preliminary inference and visual explanation infrastructure using attention, Grad-CAM, confidence scoring, and medical-term extraction.
   - Next step is controlled faithfulness evaluation, not clinical deployment.

5. **Proposed Framework**
   Include one simple figure:
   `GI image + question -> VQA model/agent -> evidence state -> validation -> answer + explanation -> faithfulness audit`

6. **Expected Contributions**
   Claim a protocol and research agenda:
   - Evaluation framework for faithful agentic medical VQA.
   - Failure-mode taxonomy for misleading explanations, memory updates, and tool use.
   - Roadmap from VQA baseline to agentic clinical reasoning audit.

## PhD Roadmap

**Phase 1: Now to 6 months**
Turn the notebook into a reproducible VQA baseline and faithfulness pilot. Run deletion tests, random-region controls, confidence shifts, and attention/Grad-CAM comparisons.  
Submission target: UKAIRS poster, MediaEval/Medico write-up, MICCAI or XAI workshop.

**Phase 2: 6 to 12 months**
Develop the core VLM explanation faithfulness paper. Compare visual explanations, language rationales, and multimodal explanations under perturbation.  
Submission target: MICCAI, MLHC, NeurIPS/ICLR workshop, or medical XAI venue.

**Phase 3: 12 to 18 months**
Extend from standalone VQA to agentic workflows inspired by EndoAgent/EndoCogniAgent: tool use, retrieval, segmentation/localisation, memory, and self-validation. Audit intermediate states, not just final answers.  
Submission target: agentic AI workshop, MICCAI, or applied ML conference.

**Phase 4: 18 to 24 months**
Add counterfactual and hallucination-oriented evaluation. Use MedHEval-style categories: visual misinterpretation, knowledge deficiency, and context misalignment. Test whether agents detect or amplify these failures.  
Submission target: stronger conference paper or journal short paper.

**Phase 5: 24 to 30 months**
Run a clinician-facing calibration study: answer-only vs answer-plus-visual-explanation vs answer-plus-multimodal-explanation. Measure error detection, over-reliance, and confidence calibration.  
Submission target: TMI, MedIA, CHI, or a thesis-synthesis journal paper.

## Test Plan

For the UKAIRS submission, verify:
- PDF uses the ACM `sigconf` template and stays within 2 main pages, with references on an extra page.
- Claims match current evidence: preliminary model/explanation infrastructure, not completed clinical validation.
- The figure is readable in two-column format and has an accessibility description.
- References include Kvasir-VQA-x1, Medico 2025, MedHEval, and EndoAgent/EndoCogniAgent.

For the PhD work, evaluate:
- VQA performance: BLEU, ROUGE, METEOR, exact/clinical answer correctness.
- Visual faithfulness: targeted deletion vs random deletion.
- Cross-modal grounding: pathology terms linked to image regions.
- Agentic robustness: corrupted tools, shifted masks, wrong retrieved cases, stale memory.
- Human safety: clinician error detection and over-reliance.
